Problem & Objective
⚠ Problem

Traditional wrist pulse diagnosis requires extensive clinical training and produces subjective, inconsistent results across practitioners. Existing commercial wearable devices capable of accurate PPG signal acquisition are prohibitively expensive, limiting accessibility — particularly in developing markets.

✦ Objective

Design and implement a low-cost wearable device that acquires photoplethysmography (PPG) signals with clinically relevant accuracy, enabling objective cardiovascular disease screening. Total target BOM cost: under 1,300 EGP vs. commercial alternatives at 2,000+ EGP.

🔗 Product Engineering Relevance: This project demonstrates full product development lifecycle — from requirements definition and component selection through BOM costing, PCB design, 3D-printed enclosure, embedded firmware, and mobile UI — directly reflecting the industrialization and product structuring workflow required at Schréder.
Hardware Components

ESP32 Microcontroller
Dual-Core + Wi-Fi + Bluetooth
Reads sensor data, processes PPG signals, and transmits wirelessly to the mobile application via Bluetooth. Main intelligence hub of the wearable.
Ultra-Low Power BT + Wi-Fi Rich I/O
BIOSIGNAL SENSOR
MAX30102
Pulse Oximeter & Heart-Rate Sensor
Integrated optical sensor with internal LEDs and photodetectors for non-invasive PPG acquisition. Operates on 1.8V supply with I2C communication. Designed for wearable health monitoring.
1.8V Supply I2C Interface Ambient Light Rejection
DISPLAY
OLED Display
128×64 px — I2C
High-contrast organic LED display for local data readout. No backlight required — reduces power consumption significantly. Shows real-time PPG waveform and measurements on-device.
128×64 px No Backlight I2C Bus
POWER
Li-Po Battery
Rechargeable 3.7V Cell
Compact rechargeable power source enabling fully wireless, portable operation. Sized for extended wearable use without frequent recharging.
PCB
Custom PCB
Designed & Fabricated
Custom-designed printed circuit board integrating all components in a compact wearable footprint. Replaces breadboard prototype for reliable, production-representative testing.
ENCLOSURE
3D Printed Housing
Dual Layout Design
Custom wrist-worn enclosure designed in two layout variants, 3D printed for physical prototype validation. Ensures correct sensor positioning against the wrist for consistent PPG acquisition.

Key Outcomes
  • ◆ Designed and delivered a complete wearable IoT product — from initial requirements through component selection, BOM costing, PCB design, 3D-printed housing, firmware development, and mobile app integration.
  • ◆ Achieved 36% cost reduction versus commercial equivalents (1,275 EGP vs. 2,000+ EGP) through strategic component selection and local manufacturing — demonstrating cost-engineering awareness directly applicable to product industrialization.
  • ◆ Successfully acquired and validated PPG signals across 5 patient age groups (22–62 years), confirming device accuracy and reliability for cardiovascular screening applications.
  • ◆ Implemented wireless data pipeline — ESP32 to Flutter mobile app via Bluetooth — enabling real-time remote monitoring, analogous to IoT-integrated smart infrastructure management.
  • ◆ Applied two-variant hardware design process for the 3D-printed enclosure, reflecting iterative product development methodology and DFM (Design for Manufacturability) principles.
